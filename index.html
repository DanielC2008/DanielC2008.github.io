Since graduating Belmont University with a degree in Sociology, I have bounced around trying to find a career that could utilize my love for social theory and knowledge of community research and development. I knew I was not cut out to work for the government or some giant think tank, the normal routes presented to Sociology majors, so I decided I would pave my own way. I would search until I found the best fit. So far I have worked in many fields including healthcare, food service, event planning, construction, and sales. In every field I have made an impact, but none seemed to feed my desire to integrate what I learned at Belmont as well as a deep love that I was born with... a love for solving problems.
In January of 2016, I learned that Nashville was looking for dedicated problem solvers. Specifically, problem solvers who had the drive to go from passive software user to dynamic software pioneer! So, in May I joined Nashville Software School. Immediately, I realized that this new world is where I belong. It was like walking into an alternate universe where my coworkers actually cared about the process as much as the result. Many were like me... completely unsatisfied until the answer was uncovered. Problem solving absorbs much of my life from video games, sports, research papers, questions about physics, politics, and even teaching. My innate curiosity about the world around me was my first clue that I could survive in a world that is about finding new solutions to old problems. Add to that the social aspect that surrounds the developer culture and all that developers create!
Software Development prompts it's students to continuously hone their craft and disseminate knowledge. I'm excited to have found a career that actually encourages me to be a life long learner gives me a chance to use my degree and my greatest passion!

Sky-Pie is a Node/Express app utilizing WEB-RTC and Socket.io for real-time video conferencing and text chat. Working with sockets has been my favorite experience with code yet! The flow chart on my Github shows the step by step connection of two browsers and the tools that help that connection happen. Feel free to check out the hosted version here to chat with someone!
My Condition Tracker is a CRUD app that allows users to store statistical data for Dungeons & Dragons' 4th Edition: "Pathfinder". "Pathfinder" is a table top role playing game that deals with lots of data. My Condition Tracker allows users to manage and manipulate that data in real time. By clicking the character's image, you can automatically add "conditions" that may befall the hero. It is hosted on firebase. Click the image below to check it out or go straight to the code!
